🍷 Liquor Licence Application Assistance

We guide you through the entire liquor licensing process – from application to approval.

Get Started →

Do you need a liquor licence?

If you plan to sell alcohol – whether in a restaurant, bar, bottle store, tavern, or even a spaza shop – you need a valid liquor licence. Operating without one can lead to heavy fines, closure, and even criminal charges. We simplify the process and handle the paperwork so you can focus on your business.

📌 What We Do

  • Assess which licence type you need
  • Complete all application forms
  • Prepare the required supporting documents
  • Guide you on zoning and municipal compliance
  • Submit your application to the Liquor Board
  • Follow up and track progress until approval

💰 Pricing

R 750 (fixed fee – excludes government fees paid by you)

Turnaround: 3‑5 days for preparation; Liquor Board processing 2‑4 months.

You pay the Liquor Board fees directly – we handle the rest.

📄 What You Need to Provide

  • Certified copy of your ID (or company registration)
  • Proof of business registration (CIPC)
  • Proof of premises ownership or lease agreement
  • Zoning certificate (we can assist)
  • Floor plan of the premises
  • Police clearance (we can assist)

🍷 Types of Liquor Licences

On‑Consumption

For restaurants, bars, pubs, and hotels where customers drink on the premises.

Off‑Consumption

For bottle stores, supermarkets, and liquor stores where customers take alcohol away.

Tavern / Shebeen

For community‑based alcohol outlets, often in residential areas.

Wholesale / Distribution

For businesses that supply alcohol to other licensees (restaurants, bars, etc.).

Event / Temporary

For festivals, markets, weddings, and one‑off events where alcohol is sold.

Micro‑Manufacturer

For craft breweries, distilleries, and wineries producing small batches.

Important Information

  • Liquor licences are issued by the Provincial Liquor Board – each province has different requirements.
  • Processing times vary – we'll help you track your application.
  • You must display your licence prominently at your premises at all times.
  • Operating without a licence is a criminal offence with severe penalties.
  • We are not lawyers – but we guide you through the process and prepare all documentation.
